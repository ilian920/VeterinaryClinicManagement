## Introduction
In today's world, the importance of veterinary clinic management systems cannot be overstated. With a rapidly increasing pet population and the complexities involved in pet care, it's essential for veterinary clinics to adopt efficient management solutions. These systems not only streamline operations but also enhance communication between clinic staff, ensuring that pet owners receive the best possible care for their animals. As the veterinary field evolves, so too must the tools and systems we use to manage it effectively.

## 2.1 Goals and Objectives
The primary goal of this project is to develop a comprehensive management system tailored for veterinary clinics. The objectives include: 1) Reducing administrative burdens on veterinary staff, 2) Enhancing service delivery to pet owners, 3) Facilitating easy access to medical records and appointment scheduling, and 4) Ensuring compliance with regulatory standards in veterinary care.

## 2.2 Target Audience
The system is aimed at several key groups: clinic owners, who require efficient management tools; veterinarians, who need quick access to patient records and scheduling tools; administrative staff, who will benefit from streamlined processes; and pet owners, who seek timely updates and records concerning their pets' health.

## Chapter 3 Design and Architecture
This system adopts the MVC architecture, which separates concerns for an efficient workflow. The layered structure includes:
- Shared
- Data
- Services
- Presentation

The database design features 7 key entities: Users, Veterinarians, Animals, Appointments, Vet Services, Medical Records, and Vaccinations, ensuring comprehensive management of all aspects of clinic operations.
